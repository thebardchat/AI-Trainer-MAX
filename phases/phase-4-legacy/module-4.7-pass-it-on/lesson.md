## KEY TERMS

- **YourNameBrain**: A personal AI system loaded with your knowledge, values, memories, and voice. It runs on your hardware, holds your data, and can answer questions about who you are and what you stood for — even when you are not in the room.

- **Legacy Knowledge**: Entries in the knowledge base tagged with categories like "family" or "philosophy." These are the beliefs, principles, and lessons you want preserved. Not trivia — truth.

- **Personal Vault**: Your private document store. Medical records, important letters, life stories, recipes, instructions — anything you would put in a fireproof safe, except this one is searchable by meaning.

- **RAG Conversation**: Retrieval-Augmented Generation. When you talk to your brain, it searches everything you taught it, pulls the relevant pieces, and generates a response using Ollama. It does not guess. It draws from what you gave it.

- **Letter to My Children**: A vault document and knowledge entry that captures what you want your family to know. The AI stores it, indexes it, and can surface it when someone asks the right question years from now.

## THE LESSON

### What Is a YourNameBrain?

Think of a family Bible. Not the words printed in it — the handwritten notes in the margins. The births recorded in the front. The names underlined. The pressed flowers. The letter tucked inside the cover.

A YourNameBrain is that — but alive. It does not just store your words. It understands them well enough to answer questions about them. Your grandchild can sit at a terminal and ask, "What did Grandpa believe about hard work?" and get an answer in your voice, drawn from the values you put in.

ShaneBrain is the living example. It holds knowledge about family, faith, fatherhood, sobriety, and building things that matter. It knows the people in Shane's life. It can write in his voice. It is not a backup. It is a continuation.

You are about to build yours.

### The Three Layers

A YourNameBrain has three layers:

**1. Values (Knowledge Base)**
These are the principles you live by. "Family comes first." "Show up every day." "Never lie to your children." "Hard work is not punishment — it is proof you care." You add these as knowledge entries with the category "family" or "philosophy." They become the foundation the AI draws from when it speaks.

**2. Stories (Personal Vault)**
These are the memories, lessons, and documents that give your values weight. Not "I believe in hard work" — but "The summer I was sixteen, my father handed me a shovel and said we were building a retaining wall. It took three weekends. I hated every minute. Twenty years later I built my own house and thanked him." Stories are what make values real.

**3. Voice (Conversation)**
When someone talks to your brain, the AI pulls from your values and stories and generates a response. The more you feed it, the more it sounds like you. Not a copy. Not a chatbot wearing your name. A reflection of what you taught it — the same way your children reflect what you taught them. Imperfect, but real.

### How to Maintain It

A YourNameBrain is not a one-time build. It is a living system.

- **Add to it regularly.** New lessons learned. New stories worth keeping. Updated values as you grow.
- **Talk to it.** Ask it questions. See if the answers sound right. If they do not, feed it better content.
- **Back it up.** Your Weaviate data lives on your hardware. Back up the Docker volumes. Copy them to a second drive. Treat them like family photos.
- **Share access.** When the time comes, your family needs to know how to start Ollama, start Weaviate, and talk to the brain. Write those instructions down. Put them in the vault.

### How to Pass It On

The hardest part of legacy is not building it. It is making sure someone receives it.

1. **Document the startup.** A simple text file: "Start Docker. Start Ollama. Run this script." Your family does not need to understand vectors. They need to know which buttons to press.
2. **Store the hardware instructions.** Which drive has the data? What is the password? Where is the backup? Put this in the vault itself — the brain can tell them.
3. **Write the letter.** Every YourNameBrain should have at least one letter addressed to the people who will inherit it. Not instructions — heart. Why you built it. What you hope they find in it. What you want them to know.
4. **Test it with family.** Sit your kids down. Let them ask it questions. See what it says. Fix what sounds wrong. Add what is missing.
